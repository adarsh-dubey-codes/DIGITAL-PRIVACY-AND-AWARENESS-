The Problem

India is Online.
But Are We Safe?

With 85+ crore internet users, India is the world's second-largest online market. Yet most users don't know basic cyber safety rules — making them easy targets.

😰
Lack of Awareness
70% of Indian internet users cannot identify a phishing email or fake website. Scammers exploit this ignorance daily.
📱
Risky App Behaviour
Average Indian has 80+ apps on phone, many with unnecessary access to camera, mic, location & contacts.
🌐
Rural Digital Divide
First-time internet users in rural areas are especially vulnerable — with no safety education available in local languages.
🔐
Weak Passwords
"123456" and "password" are still India's most used passwords in 2024. One breach can expose banking, email, and social media.

Privacy Awareness Modules

Simple, practical lessons. No jargon. Just real tips you can use right now.

🔑 Password Safety

Your password is the lock on your digital home. A weak lock invites burglars. Learn to create passwords that even hackers can't crack.

✅
Use 12+ characters — Mix UPPERCASE, lowercase, numbers & symbols like @, #, $
🚫
Never use your name, birthday, "123456", or "password" — these are cracked in seconds
🔄
Different password for each app — If Gmail is hacked, your bank should still be safe
📱
Use a Password Manager — Apps like Bitwarden (free) remember passwords so you don't have to
🔐
Enable 2-Factor Authentication (2FA) — Even if your password leaks, hackers can't login without your OTP

🎣 Phishing Detection

Phishing is when criminals create fake websites or emails that look real — to steal your password or money. Learn to spot them instantly.

🔍
Check the URL carefully — "sbi-secure.com" is NOT "sbi.co.in". Look for the official domain.
🔒
Look for HTTPS + padlock — No padlock = not secure. Never enter passwords without it.
⚡
Urgency = Red Flag — "Your account will be blocked in 2 hours!" is a manipulation tactic.
📧
Check sender email — support@amaz0n.in is NOT amazon.com. Look for typos.
🛑
When in doubt, don't click — Go directly to the official website by typing the URL yourself.
Real or Fake? — Click to Reveal
🌐 https://www.sbi.co.in/web/personal-banking
✅ SAFE — Official SBI domain (.co.in)
🌐 http://sbi-kyc-update.in/login.php
🚨 FAKE — Not SBI's domain, no HTTPS, suspicious path
🌐 https://paytm-reward-claim.com/win500
🚨 FAKE — "reward-claim" domain is not Paytm
🌐 https://www.irctc.co.in/nget/train-search
✅ SAFE — Official IRCTC government domain

📲 OTP & Scam Awareness

OTP (One Time Password) is your secret key. Once you share it, criminals can empty your bank account within minutes.

🔴
NEVER share OTP — Not with bank, not with family, not with "RBI officer". OTP is only for YOU.
📞
Banks NEVER call for OTP — If someone claiming to be from your bank asks for OTP, hang up immediately.
💳
Beware of "Reverse Transaction" trick — "Please accept ₹500" requests can DEDUCT money from your account.
🎁
Lottery you didn't enter = Scam — KBC, government lottery — all fake. Ignore and block.
📱
Don't install remote apps — "AnyDesk" or "TeamViewer" requests from strangers give them full control of your phone.
Real SMS — Real or Scam?
📱 "Your SBI OTP is 847291. Do not share with anyone. Valid for 10 min. - SBI"
✅ REAL — Came from bank system. Never share this OTP!
📞 Caller: "Sir, main SBI se bol raha hoon. Aapka account block ho jayega. OTP batao."
🚨 SCAM — Banks NEVER ask OTP on call. Hang up & call 1930!
📱 "Congratulations! You've won ₹25 Lakh in KBC. Click: bit.ly/kbc-win to claim."
🚨 SCAM — KBC never contacts winners like this. Block & delete!
📱 "OTP for login to IRCTC is 556123. If not requested by you, do not share. - IRCTC"
✅ REAL — System-generated OTP. Only use it yourself, never share!

📱 Social Media Privacy

Your social media profile is like a public noticeboard. What you share there can be used by strangers, hackers, and even employers.

🔒
Set profile to Private — Instagram, Facebook: go to Settings → Privacy → Private Account.
📍
Never share live location — "We're at Mumbai airport" tells thieves your house is empty.
🧾
Don't share Aadhaar/PAN photos — Even "to prove identity" to strangers online. Use government portals only.
👥
Reject unknown friend requests — Fake profiles are created to gather your personal information.
🤔
Think before you post — Vacation photos posted live can attract burglars. Post after returning home.

⚖️ Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDPA) 2023 — Your Rights
Right to Information
You can ask any company what personal data they hold about you and why they're using it.
Right to Correction
You can demand correction of inaccurate personal data held by any data fiduciary.
Right to Erasure
Request deletion of your personal data when it's no longer needed for the original purpose.
Right to Grievance
Lodge complaints with the Data Protection Board of India for violations of your data rights.
Consent Required
Companies must get your clear consent before collecting or processing your personal data.
Children's Protection
Special protections for data of children under 18. Parental consent mandatory.
